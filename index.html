About me

Not just a coder who also mentors.

I've worked across startups and big teams, worn a lot of hats, and made a lot of mistakes. The good kind. The kind that teach you things textbooks skip over.

These days I split my time between consulting on technical problems, doing 1:1 mentorship with engineers figuring out their next move, and building communities where people actually talk to each other honestly.

I'm based in the UK, but work with people everywhere.

System design Career strategy Team leadership Full-stack Engineering culture Distributed systems Public speaking

I'll be straight with you

No sugar-coating. If something isn't working, I'll say so, kindly but clearly. Most people find that more useful than reassurance.

Tech should be more open

I've benefited from people who shared their knowledge freely. I try to do the same.

It's not just about code

The stuff that really holds engineers back is rarely technical. It's confidence, politics, communication, timing. That's where I spend a lot of our time.

I like this work

Genuinely. The moment someone gets a promotion they didn't think was possible, or ships something they're proud of. That never gets old.

Community & speaking

Born in India, building community in the UK.

I came to the UK as an international student and have since worked across finance, travel, product, services, and cybersecurity. Along the way, I realised the best things in my career came from the people around me, not just the code I wrote.

That's why I'm involved in community building with NN1 Dev Club and the University of Northampton, helping connect developers, students, and makers across Northamptonshire and beyond.

I'm always looking for more opportunities to speak, present, and run workshops. If you're organising an event, I'd love to hear from you.
