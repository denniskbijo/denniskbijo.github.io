For international engineers

Visa sponsorship in the UK: what I wish I'd known.

I've navigated the UK visa sponsorship process twice. It's stressful, confusing, and full of conflicting advice. Here's what actually helped me.

Companies that sponsor

Not every company can or will sponsor. Focus your search on those with a track record.

  • Check the UK Register of Licensed Sponsors on GOV.UK
  • Target large companies (banks, consultancies, tech firms) that sponsor regularly
  • Barclays, Capital One, JP Morgan, Dell, Ocado and many others actively sponsor Skilled Worker visas
  • Smaller companies can sponsor too, but check their licence status first

Tips for the process

The process is manageable if you prepare well and stay organised.

  • Start applying early. Sponsorship takes time on the employer's side
  • Be upfront about your visa needs. Good employers won't be put off
  • Use AI tools to tailor your CV for each application
  • Network at university events. My Barclays job came from a career session

Common mistakes

Things I see mentees get wrong repeatedly.

  • Applying only to companies that don't sponsor
  • Not mentioning sponsorship until the final interview stage
  • Ignoring the Graduate Visa route as a bridge to sponsored employment
  • Underestimating how long the Home Office processing takes

Useful resources

Bookmark these if you're going through the process.

  • GOV.UK Skilled Worker visa page for official guidance
  • Register of Licensed Sponsors to check employer licences
  • UKCISA for international student support
  • My MentorCruise profile for 1:1 help with the process
